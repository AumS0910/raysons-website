Our Journey

Growth & innovation
since 1987.

Forty years, ten milestones. Scroll to travel the corridor — from the first Resin Coated Sand in Maharashtra to a full-service precision casting partner.

Era I · Foundation

The first RCS
in Maharashtra.

1987

Foundation of Raysons Industries

Established as the first manufacturer of Resin Coated Sand (RCS) in Maharashtra — pioneering a new era of quality foundry materials in the region.

1992

Expansion with SBR Unit

A second manufacturing unit set up under SBR to meet growing demand, expanding production capacity and customer reach.

1995

ISO Certification

Raysons SBR achieved ISO certification — a commitment to international quality standards and consistent process excellence.

1987
Era II · Expansion

Scaling the
sand business.

1997

Inception of SRI Durga Mata

Diversified operations, further strengthening our position in the foundry and allied industries.

2002

Launch of RR Sand Industries

Founded to support growing requirements for bonding and coating materials — another specialised division under the group umbrella.

2005

Raysons Sand Chem Pvt. Ltd.

Commissioned a state-of-the-art plant with 3,000 tons/month capacity, serving larger domestic and export markets.

2005
Era III · Ignition

The foundry
is lit.

2021

Establishment of Raysons Shell Cast

Founded with a 450-ton monthly melting capacity — our entry into precision shell moulded casting manufacturing for leading global industries.

2022

No-Bake Foundry Line Commissioned

A modern No-Bake line to meet demand for larger, complex castings — expanding technical capabilities and production flexibility.

2021
Era IV · The Road Ahead

Full-service
precision partner.

2026

Advanced Machine Shop & Heat Treatment

A dedicated CNC/VMC machine shop and in-house heat treatment facility — enabling fully machined, ready-to-assemble casting deliveries.

2027

New HMC & VMC Machine Additions

Further expansion of machining capabilities — cementing Raysons as a full-service precision casting partner.

Our People

Operators are trained through process discipline, shop-floor supervision, inspection feedback and repeatable work standards — accountability from raw material to shipment.
